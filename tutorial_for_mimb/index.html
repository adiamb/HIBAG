Introduction

SNP-based imputation approaches for human leukocyte antigen (HLA) typing take advantage of the extended haplotype structure within the MHC to predict classical HLA alleles using dense SNP genotypes, such as those available from SNP panels of genome- wide association study (GWAS). These methods enable HLA analyses on existing SNP datasets genotyped in GWAS studies at no extra cost. Here, I describe the workflow of HIBAG for obtaining a sample’s HLA class I and II genotypes of two-field resolution using SNP data. Two examples are provided to illustrate with a publicly available HLA and SNP dataset: genotype imputation with pre-fit classifiers in GWAS, and model training to build a new classifier.
